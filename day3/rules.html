The Rules of Blackjack

Blackjack is a popular card game where the goal is to have a hand value closer to 21 than the dealer's without exceeding 21. Here are the key rules:

How to Play Blackjack

1. Card Values

  • Number cards (2-10) are worth their face value.
  • Face cards (Jack, Queen, King) are worth 10 points each.
  • Aces can be worth 1 or 11 points, whichever benefits the player.

2. Gameplay

  • Each player starts with two cards, as does the dealer (one of the dealer's cards is face-up).
  • Players can choose to:
    • Hit – Draw another card.
    • Stand – Keep their current hand.
    • Double Down – Double the bet, take one more card, and stand.
    • Split – If the first two cards are a pair, split them into two hands with an extra bet.
    • Surrender – In some versions, forfeit half the bet and quit.

3. Winning the Game

  • A hand totaling 21 with the first two cards (Ace + 10-value card) is a Blackjack and typically pays 3:2.
  • If a player's hand exceeds 21, they bust and lose.
  • After all players finish, the dealer reveals their hidden card and must hit until they reach at least 17.
  • The player wins if their total is higher than the dealer’s or if the dealer busts.
  • If both have the same total, it's a push (tie), and the bet is returned.

Warning

Busting:

Be careful! If you exceed 21, you "bust" and automatically lose the round.
